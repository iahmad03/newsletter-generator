Two Amazon Prime Air drones crashed in Arizona

Two Amazon delivery drones reportedly collided with a crane this morning in Tolleson, AZ. No injuries have been reported. The incident occurred about two miles from a fulfillment center run by the tech company and involved two of its Prime Air drones. The Federal Aviation Administration will be taking over the investigation."We're currently working with the relevant authorities to investigate," an Amazon representative said. "We're aware of an incident involving two Prime Air drone in Tolleson, Arizona," Amazon representative Terrence Clark said.

‘Failed Star’ Mimics a Key Sign of Life—and That’s Bad News for Alien Hunters

Astronomers report the first-ever detection of strong phosphine signatures on a brown dwarf. The phosphine detected on this brown dwarf, named Wolf 1130C, almost certainly formed through natural, abiotic processes. The challenge now is figuring out how an object like this could generate so much of it without life. Until researchers can explain that, any detection of phosphine can’t be taken as a reliable sign of biology. The next step would be to look for other objects with similar supplies of phosphorus which could help fill in the gaps.
